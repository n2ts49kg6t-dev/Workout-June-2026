⚡ Your situation
At 85kg / 13% body fat you're already lean — roughly 73.5kg of muscle. Belly fat at this stage is subcutaneous and responds to a calorie deficit, not more ab work.
🔥
You can't spot-reduce belly fat
Subcutaneous fat at your level responds to overall fat loss only. No exercise targets it directly.
📉
Small calorie deficit
~250–350 kcal/day below maintenance. Aggressive cuts kill muscle at your training level. Track with MyFitnessPal for 2–3 weeks.
🥩
Protein: 136–187g/day
1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight. Preserves muscle in a deficit and keeps you full.
🏃
2× Zone 2 cardio on rest days
20–30 min brisk walk or incline treadmill. Burns fat without impairing muscle recovery.
😴
Sleep is a training variable
Poor sleep raises cortisol which directly increases visceral fat storage. 7–9 hours non-negotiable.
Upper traps respond best to heavy loaded elevation (shrugs), high-elbow pulls (face pulls, upright rows), and isometric tension (carries). All three are in your programme on Day 1 and Day 4.
Barbell / DB ShrugDay 1
Highest load capacity = most mechanical tension. Hold 1–2s at top, full depression at bottom.
Cable Face PullDay 1
Upper trap + rear delt + rotator cuff. Elbows high, pull rope to forehead. Essential for shoulder health.
Dumbbell Upright RowDay 4
Full upper trap ROM with medial delt synergy. Elbows above hands throughout the lift.
Farmer's Walks (optional)Any
Isometric trap contraction under load — grab heaviest DBs, walk 40m. Incredibly effective.
💡 Programming note
Upper traps recover fast — hitting them Day 1 and Day 4 with rest between is optimal. Full ROM on shrugs is critical: most people half-rep and miss the lower-trap stretch that drives growth.
